Almost every full-stack project I&apos;ve built has a database question at the front of it. Postgres or Mongo? It&apos;s the developer equivalent of "tabs or spaces" — except the wrong answer can cost you a migration nine months in.

Here&apos;s how I actually decide.

## My default: Postgres

For greenfield product work, **I reach for Postgres first**. It&apos;s not because Mongo is bad; it&apos;s because Postgres&apos; surface area covers so many product shapes that I rarely need to switch.

What I get out of the box:

- Real transactions, real foreign keys, real constraints.
- JSON columns when I genuinely need schemaless.
- Full-text search good enough to ship before you reach for OpenSearch.
- A community of tools (Prisma, Drizzle, Kysely, PostgREST) that&apos;s deeper than Mongo&apos;s.

> Schemas aren&apos;t a tax. They&apos;re a contract between your past self and your future self.

## When I&apos;d still pick Mongo

I&apos;m not anti-Mongo. There are shapes where it genuinely wins:

- **High-churn document shapes** where the schema is genuinely fluid (CMS-style content, event payloads).
- **Realtime apps** with collaborative editing where MongoDB&apos;s Change Streams + Atlas Search are a comfortable stack.
- **Existing teams** with Mongo expertise. Familiarity beats theoretical optimums.

## The deciding question

When a founder asks me, I ask back one question: _"Does your data have relationships you&apos;d describe with the word `belongs to`?"_

If yes → Postgres.
If you have to think → Postgres.
If no → either works, default to whichever your team knows.

That&apos;s the heuristic. Everything else is implementation detail.
