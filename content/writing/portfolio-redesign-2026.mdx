You&apos;re reading this on the second version of my portfolio. The first one shipped in 2023 — Next 13, Tailwind 3, Framer Motion 10 — and lasted almost three years before it started to feel _of its moment_ instead of timeless.

This is the brief I gave myself, and what I built.

## The brief

**Aesthetic:** dark, editorial, premium. The kind of site that says _engineer who reads design books_, not _engineer who installed a template_.

**Stack:** the 2026 default — Next 15, React 19, Tailwind v4, Motion 11. I also added React Three Fiber for the hero scene, Lenis for smooth scroll, and a small MDX-powered writing system (this post is rendered from a file on disk).

**Anti-goals:**

- No "I&apos;m available for freelance!" loud banner. Status is a quiet pill.
- No giant skill cloud with 47 logos. Three categorized groups, that&apos;s it.
- No animation for its own sake. If it doesn&apos;t serve reading or scanning, it&apos;s cut.

## What I&apos;m proud of

- The hero&apos;s scroll-driven parallax on the headline.
- The bento grid for projects — different sizes guide the eye in priority order.
- The command palette (try `⌘K`). Engineers will recognize it immediately.
- The scroll-driven progress fill on the timeline.

## What I&apos;d still cut if I had to

The 3D blob. It&apos;s pretty, but it&apos;s 400KB of JavaScript for decoration. If load metrics start to matter, it goes.

> Every portfolio is a quiet argument about how you work. This one argues for restraint.
